Page 01 / 06 · The Visible Tactics

The extractions you can name.

Some energy harvesting is visible — not always in the moment, but identifiable within minutes of the interaction ending, once you know what to listen for. These are the tactics that operate above the waterline. They draw from specific channels, they produce recognisable body signatures, and they are the easiest to interrupt once you have the language for them.

An important caveat before the naming: the people running these tactics are not always conscious of what they are doing. Most energy extraction is unconscious — a learned pattern, often installed in childhood, that the person runs automatically in the same way you run your shape from Course Two. Naming the tactic is not the same as accusing the person. The tactic is a structure. The structure can be identified without prosecuting the person running it.

Tactic 01

The crisis-dump.

They arrive with urgency already in motion. The conversation begins, without preamble, at emotional intensity — a crisis that requires your immediate attention, your emotional bandwidth, your nervous system's full activation. You had other plans for this energy. The plans have been quietly cancelled by the arrival of the crisis. The crisis may be real. The extraction is in the timing, the absence of invitation, and the assumption that your bandwidth is available.

Channels drawn from: emotional bandwidth, nervous system capacity, attention
The felt signature: you are suddenly inside someone else's emergency without having consented to enter it. Your breathing shifts. Your own thoughts scatter. The afternoon you had planned is now occupied by material you did not choose to carry.
Tactic 02

The attention monopoly.

The conversation runs in one direction for the duration. They talk. You listen. The listening is not shared — when you speak, the pause is a waiting room rather than a genuine reception. Your contribution is received as an interruption rather than as part of the exchange. Over thirty, sixty, ninety minutes, your attention has been consumed without a single moment of reciprocal engagement. The monopoly is often invisible to the person running it. They experienced the conversation as connection. You experienced it as occupation.

Channel drawn from: attention
The felt signature: you leave the interaction feeling like you have been watched but not seen. You were present the entire time. You were never, at any point, met.
Tactic 03

The unsolicited correction.

They offer advice, instruction, or correction that you did not ask for — about your work, your body, your choices, your relationships, your life direction. The correction is framed as care, as expertise, as the concern of someone who knows better. The extraction is not in the content of the advice. It is in the positioning — you have been placed, without consenting to the placement, in the role of the one who needs correcting. The placement draws from your sense of authority over your own life. The channel being harvested is not attention. It is creative and personal sovereignty.

Channel drawn from: sexual/creative presence (the life-force channel)
The felt signature: you leave the interaction feeling vaguely diminished, as though something about your capacity to make your own decisions has been quietly questioned. You cannot always point to what was said that produced this. The production was structural, not verbal.
Tactic 04

The emotional audit.

They require you to account for your emotional state — to explain why you are quiet, to justify your mood, to produce a narrative that makes your inner life legible to them. The audit is not curiosity. Curiosity asks and accepts the answer. The audit asks and then evaluates the answer against a standard you did not agree to. The extraction is the cost of being required to translate your inner life into a performance of your inner life, for an audience that will then assess the performance.

Channels drawn from: emotional bandwidth, nervous system capacity
The felt signature: you have been made to perform an emotion you were privately processing in order to satisfy someone else's need to understand it. The processing, which was yours, has been converted into a production for them.
The visible tactics are not hidden. They are operating in plain sight, unnamed. The naming does not make you paranoid. It makes you literate — able to read, in real time, what is happening to your energy while it is happening.
Scenario · Sit with this

A colleague arrives at your desk and begins, without preamble, describing a crisis in their personal life.

You can feel the energy shift instantly — your shoulders tighten, your focus scatters, your own work recedes. The crisis is real. The extraction began before you had time to decide whether to give.

Page 02 / 06 · The Invisible Tactics

The extractions that operate below awareness.

The visible tactics can be named in the moment, once you have language for them. The invisible tactics cannot — they operate below the waterline of conscious awareness, producing their effects without ever being seen directly. You feel the depletion afterwards. You cannot, in the moment, identify the mechanism. This is what makes the invisible tactics more damaging: they bypass your governance entirely, because the extraction occurs in a register you were not monitoring.

There are four invisible tactics that this module names. Each one is felt in the body rather than observed in the interaction. Each one produces a specific signature that, once described, becomes recognisable in retrospect — and eventually, with practice, in real time.

Tactic 05

Ambient mood transfer.

They do not tell you they are anxious, angry, or distressed. They simply are. The mood radiates into the shared space without ever being declared. You absorb it without noticing the absorption — the way you absorb temperature from a room without deciding to. Ten minutes later, you are carrying an emotion that was not yours when you entered. The transfer is not deliberate. It is the nervous system's natural tendency to co-regulate — except in this case, the regulation is one-directional. They stabilise. You destabilise. Neither of you witnessed the exchange.

Channel drawn from: nervous system capacity
The felt signature: an emotion that was not in you before the interaction, with no identifiable origin. A heaviness, a restlessness, an irritability that arrived without a thought attached to it. You carry it as if it were yours. It was not yours when you walked in.
Tactic 06

The occupation of your thinking.

Hours after the interaction, you find yourself still thinking about them — replaying the conversation, rehearsing what you should have said, wondering what they meant, analysing their motives. The thinking is not invited. It arrives and it stays, occupying bandwidth you had allocated to other things. The occupation is the extraction. The person is no longer present, but they are consuming your attention as surely as if they were sitting across from you. The taking continues after the encounter has ended — which is the signature of extraction rather than chosen exchange.

Channel drawn from: attention, creative presence
The felt signature: a loop of thinking about someone that you cannot easily exit. The thinking is not productive — it does not resolve or clarify. It simply occupies. The occupying is the cost.
Tactic 07

The uninvited sexual gaze.

This is the first tactic in this course that draws specifically from the fourth channel — sexual and creative presence. The uninvited sexual gaze is the experience of being looked at with sexual attention that you did not invite, consent to, or want. The gaze may last a moment. The extraction can last hours. What is being taken is not your comfort. What is being taken is a piece of your sexual presence — your right to be in your body, in that moment, without your body being consumed by someone else's attention.

This tactic is gendered in its distribution but not in its mechanism. Women experience it more frequently and more persistently. Men experience it more rarely and are more likely to dismiss it when it occurs. The mechanism is the same: your body has been entered, through the channel of sexual attention, without your governance having been consulted. The cost is felt as a tightening, a shrinking, a sudden self-consciousness about your own physical presence. The cost is the loss of a few degrees of ease in your own body.

Channel drawn from: sexual/creative presence
The felt signature: a contraction. You were at ease in your body, and now you are not. Something has shifted. You may not be able to identify the source immediately. The contraction is the body's report that the fourth channel has been drawn from.
Tactic 08

The praise that positions.

A compliment that, underneath the flattery, installs a hierarchy. You are being praised — and the praise positions them as the one who evaluates. The extraction is in the positioning, not the words. You have been made, through the praise, into someone whose value has just been assessed by someone who assumed the authority to assess it. The assessment is favourable. The cost is that the assessment occurred at all — that your body, your appearance, your work, your presence was treated as something to be graded, with the grading party operating from a position above you.

Channel drawn from: sexual/creative presence, attention
The felt signature: a small wrongness underneath a compliment. You were praised but you do not feel larger. You feel, somehow, assessed. The assessment, regardless of its outcome, is the extraction.
The invisible tactics do not announce themselves. They are felt as aftereffects — emotions that arrived from nowhere, thinking that will not stop, contractions in the body that have no obvious cause. The aftereffects are the evidence. The evidence is in you.
Scenario · Sit with this

You leave a meeting and realise, twenty minutes later, that you are carrying an irritability that was not yours before you entered the room.

The meeting was normal. Nobody was hostile. But one person in the room was quietly furious about something unrelated, and the fury radiated without being declared. You absorbed it. It is now operating in you as if it were your own.

Page 03 / 06 · The Systemic Tactics

Extraction that operates at the level of culture.

The first two pages named tactics that operate between individuals. This page addresses something larger and more difficult — the extraction that operates at the level of systems, institutions, and cultural norms. These are not tactics deployed by a single person. They are structures that harvest energy from entire populations, often so normalised that the harvesting is invisible to both the people being harvested and the people benefiting.

This is the page where the course's subtitle — Truth Reversal Framework — becomes explicit. A truth reversal is a belief that has been installed so thoroughly, across so many generations, that the reversal of a natural truth has come to feel like the truth itself. The systemic tactics in this page are all sustained by truth reversals — beliefs about bodies, sexuality, availability, and value that feel natural because they have been running long enough to feel like the way things are.

The availability norm.

The reversal: you have been told that being available — emotionally, physically, sexually — is a positive trait. That openness is warmth. That accessibility is generosity. That a person who is hard to reach is cold, aloof, withholding. The truth underneath: the availability norm serves the people who want access to your energy, not the person whose energy it is. When availability is positioned as a virtue, selectivity becomes a vice — and the governance that would protect your energy is reframed as a character flaw. The entire framing exists to keep your borders open for harvest.

The body-as-commodity norm.

The reversal: you have been told — through advertising, media, social norms, and the entire architecture of consumer culture — that your body exists in relation to how it is perceived by others. Its value is in its appearance, its desirability, its consumability. The truth underneath: your body exists in relation to how it is inhabited by you. Its value is in its capacity to carry you through a life — to feel, to create, to experience, to be present. The commodity framing converts your body from a territory you govern into a product you manage for the market. The management itself is the extraction. The hours spent on the management are hours of life-force spent on a project that serves the market, not you.

The sexual-performance norm.

The reversal: you have been told that your sexuality is a performance — something to be good at, to improve, to deploy strategically, to calibrate for the consumption of a partner or an audience. The truth underneath: your sexuality is a territory — a region of your own experience that has its own intelligence, its own rhythm, its own standards for what constitutes genuine presence versus performance. The performance norm extracts from the fourth channel by converting your sexual presence into a production for external evaluation. The production costs creative energy. The cost is paid whether or not the performance is successful.

The emotional-labour norm.

The reversal: you have been told — particularly if you are a woman — that managing other people's emotions is part of being a good person. That anticipating what others need before they ask is a gift. That the capacity to hold everyone's feelings while suppressing your own is a form of strength. The truth underneath: emotional labour, when it is expected rather than chosen, is a systemic extraction of nervous system capacity from one group of people for the comfort of another. The framing as strength is what prevents the extraction from being named. If it is your strength, you cannot also name it as their taking.

These four norms are not the only systemic extractions. They are the ones most directly relevant to this course. Each one is sustained by a truth reversal — a belief so deeply installed that its opposite feels like rebellion rather than like sanity. The course's work, in Modules Three through Six, is to examine each reversal in detail and to provide the framework for reclaiming what the reversal has been harvesting.

The most effective extraction is the one that has been reframed as a virtue. When the taking is called generosity, the governance is called selfishness, and the resistance is called coldness — the system is operating perfectly. The naming is the first disruption.

You read the four systemic norms and recognise that at least two of them have been operating in your life as unquestioned truths.

The recognition is not comfortable. The norms felt, until five minutes ago, like personal values rather than installed programming. The distinction is disorienting.

Page 04 / 06 · The Real-Time Diagnostic

Recognising extraction while it is happening.

The previous three pages gave you the catalogue — visible tactics, invisible tactics, systemic norms. This page gives you the instrument for detecting them in real time. The instrument is not a checklist you carry with you. It is a set of body-signals you learn to read while an interaction is still occurring, rather than discovering the cost only after it has been paid.

The shift is from retrospective to real-time. Module One taught you to read the body's report after interactions. This page teaches you to read the report while the interaction is still in progress — which is the precondition for governance, because governance requires awareness in the moment the extraction is occurring, not twenty minutes later when the cost has already been paid.

Seven signals the body produces during extraction
01 Breathing becomes shallow without a physical cause. You have not exercised. You are sitting still. And your breathing has shifted from deep to surface-level. The shift is the nervous system's response to being drawn from.
02 Shoulders rise. The body's instinctive guarding posture — the shoulders move upward toward the ears, the neck shortens, the chest contracts. This is the body protecting the heart space, which is the physical centre of the emotional bandwidth channel.
03 A tightening in the stomach or solar plexus. Not nausea — a drawing sensation, as though something in the core of you is being pulled forward. This signal is the most direct felt-sense report of energy moving outward without your governance.
04 Thoughts begin to scatter. You were tracking your own thinking a few minutes ago. Now the thinking is fragmented, jumping between subjects, unable to land. The scattering is the attention channel being monopolised — the cognitive bandwidth has been redirected before you noticed the redirection.
05 A contraction in your physical presence. You were taking up space a moment ago — sitting openly, gesturing normally, occupying your body. Now you have become slightly smaller. The shrinking is the body's response to identity positioning — you have been placed in a role that requires less of you than the full version.
06 The impulse to perform. You notice yourself about to say something you do not actually think, laugh at something that was not actually funny, produce an emotion you are not actually feeling. The performance impulse is the body's attempt to manage the extraction by giving the extractor what they want, in the hope that the giving will end the taking.
07 A felt sense of being consumed rather than met. This is the most comprehensive signal and the hardest to articulate. It is the difference between someone being with you and someone being at you. Being met feels mutual. Being consumed feels directional. The body knows the difference before the mind can explain it.

You do not need to detect all seven. You need to detect one. One signal, noticed while the interaction is still in progress, gives you the half-second of awareness that Module Five of Course Two described — the half-second between impulse and action. The half-second, in this context, is the moment between the extraction beginning and your governance engaging. The governance may be a small shift in your posture. It may be a boundary delivered gently. It may simply be the internal knowledge that you see what is happening, which changes the dynamic even when nothing visible changes.

You have been feeling these signals your entire life. You have been overriding them with the social narrative that tells you to stay, to give, to perform, to be available. The override is over. The signals are the governance.
Scenario · Sit with this

Midway through a conversation, you notice signal three — a drawing sensation in your stomach — and signal six — the impulse to perform a response you do not actually feel.

The conversation is socially normal. Nothing dramatic is happening. And yet your body is reporting extraction in real time. You have the awareness. The question is what to do with it.

Page 05 / 06 · The First Act

The sovereignty move.

The first act of governance is not a confrontation. It is not a boundary announced in the moment. It is not a dramatic withdrawal. It is something far simpler and far more powerful — the internal shift from "this is happening to me" to "I see what is happening and I am choosing my response." The shift is invisible to anyone else. It changes everything inside you.

This shift — from unconscious participant to conscious governor — is the fundamental move of the entire course. Everything else builds on it. You cannot govern what you cannot see. And you cannot see what you have not named. The previous four pages gave you the names. This page gives you the move.

The sovereignty move

I see what is happening. I feel the extraction in my body. I am still here, in this interaction, and I am now choosing — from governance, not from reaction — what I give, what I withhold, and how I occupy my own territory for the remainder of this exchange.

What the sovereignty move is not

It is not confrontation. The person in front of you does not need to know that you have made the shift. The shift is internal. It operates in your posture, your breathing, the quality of your attention — all of which change when you move from extraction to governance. The person may notice that something has shifted. They do not need to know what. The governance is yours, private, and does not require their permission or participation.

It is not withdrawal. You do not need to leave the interaction to make the move. You can remain in conversation, at dinner, in the meeting, at the family gathering — and govern from within. The sovereignty move is not the same as a boundary. A boundary says "this far and no further." The sovereignty move says "I am here, and I am now governing my own presence." The first is a wall. The second is a return to the chair.

It is not hardness. The sovereignty move does not require you to become cold, rigid, or defended. It requires you to become present — fully, in your own body, with your own governance engaged. Presence, properly understood, is not a barrier. It is the fullest form of availability — because it is chosen availability, given from a governed position, rather than extracted availability taken from an ungoverned one. The most generous you can be is the version of you who is giving from governance. The most depleted you can be is the version of you who is giving from extraction.

How to practise the sovereignty move

Begin with the body, not the mind.

When you detect one of the seven signals from Page Four, do not try to think your way into governance. Start with the body. Deepen the breathing — one long breath is enough. Drop the shoulders — half an inch is enough. Open the chest — a fraction is enough. These micro-adjustments are the physical form of the sovereignty move. The mind follows the body. Not the other way round.

Name the extraction silently.

In your own internal language, name what is occurring. "This is an attention monopoly." "This is a mood transfer." "This is the sexual gaze." "This is identity positioning." The naming is the move. The moment you name the tactic, you are no longer inside it — you are observing it, which is a structurally different position. The position of the observer is the position of the governor. The naming creates the position.

Choose your next response rather than producing it automatically.

After the naming, there is a beat — a half-second in which the automatic response (perform, accommodate, absorb, laugh) has been paused. In that beat, choose. You may choose to give — from governance, from consent, from the knowledge that you have the capacity and the willingness. You may choose to decline — gently, with care, without drama. Either choice is sovereign. The automaticity was the extraction. The choice is the governance.

Sovereignty is not the absence of giving. It is the presence of choosing. The same act — listening, helping, being present — is either extraction or exchange depending entirely on whether you chose it from a governed position or produced it from an automatic one. The act looks the same from outside. The cost is entirely different from inside.
Scenario · Sit with this

You are at a family dinner and notice the familiar pattern — you are managing someone's mood, performing an emotional state you do not feel, and your body is registering depletion across three channels simultaneously.

You have, for the first time in this context, named what is happening. You can feel the sovereignty move available to you. The question is whether to make it here, now, in this room, with these people.

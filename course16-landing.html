Under-18 Financial Sovereignty

Your money, your documents, your rights — the practical, legal, grown-up stuff about handling money before you turn 18.

Money can feel like an adult world you are locked out of — full of rules that seem designed to keep teenagers waiting. This course gives you the real picture: what you are genuinely entitled to, what you can do right now, and how to handle your own money safely, legally, and with confidence.

You will cover your key documents and your rights to them, locking down your digital security, opening an account that suits your age, getting paid for real work without the traps that freeze people’s money, understanding tax without the panic, and a clear plan for turning 18 — including a calm, caring plan for if home ever stops being safe.

Five modules. Honest and practical throughout. A short reflective question on every page. Written for ages 16+.

What you will cover
  • 01
    Your Documents, Your Rights
    The documents that run your financial life, your right to your own copies, and locking down the digital security that protects your money.
  • 02
    Your Own Account
    The myths about teens and banking, choosing an account that suits your age, and building money habits that stay yours.
  • 03
    Getting Paid, the Right Way
    Why payment apps say 18+, why the workarounds backfire and freeze people's money, and the legitimate routes that keep your earnings yours.
  • 04
    Tax Without the Panic
    What tax actually is, the allowances that cover most teenagers, and the five-minute habit that ends tax fear for good.
  • 05
    The 18th-Birthday Plan
    Switching on full control at 18, building credit safely without the traps, and a calm plan for if home ever stops being safe.
Time commitment: Approximately 2 hours across 5 modules. Work at your own pace — there is a reflective question on every page.
Before you begin

This course is free. No payment required.

This is general information, not financial or legal advice. Rules, figures and services change, so always check official sources like gov.uk and HMRC, and for anything important, talk to a trusted adult or a free service like Citizens Advice.

If you are dealing with money being controlled, or home ever feels unsafe, you are not alone — and it is not your fault. Free, confidential help is available from Childline (0800 1111), Citizens Advice, Shelter and Centrepoint.

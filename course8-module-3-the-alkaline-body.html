The Body Runs Alkaline

The human body is not neutral. It is designed to operate in a slightly alkaline state. Maintaining that state is one of the body's highest priorities — and what you eat either supports that work or fights against it.

pH is a measure of acidity or alkalinity, running from 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most alkaline), with 7 being neutral. Healthy human blood sits at a tightly maintained range of 7.35 to 7.45 — slightly alkaline. Even small deviations from this range indicate serious illness. The body will go to extraordinary lengths — drawing minerals from bones, producing bicarbonate, accelerating breathing — to maintain this balance.

The teaching that has guided natural healers for generations runs deeper than blood pH alone: it says that the overall internal environment — the tissues, the cells, the lymphatic system — thrives in alkalinity and struggles in chronic acidity. What you eat shifts that environment, daily and cumulatively, toward one state or the other.

Mucus, Acidity, and Disease

The healing tradition taught that all disease begins in the same place: a body pushed into chronic acidity, producing excess mucus as a protective response. Understand mucus, and you begin to understand the root of most chronic illness.

Mucus is not the enemy — it is the body's response to an acidic internal environment. When cells are under stress from acid-forming foods, toxins, and inflammation, the body produces mucus to coat and protect vulnerable tissue. This is protective in the short term. In the chronic state — when the acid-forming diet never stops — the mucus itself becomes a problem: blocking pathways, harbouring bacteria and parasites, and creating the conditions in which disease takes hold.

This framework — that disease requires an acidic, mucus-producing environment to thrive, and that an alkaline body is a hostile environment for most chronic illness — is the foundation of the alkaline healing tradition. It does not replace understanding specific conditions. It describes the terrain in which those conditions either flourish or fail to take hold.

What Makes a Food Acid-Forming

The distinction is not about whether the food tastes acidic — a lemon is acidic in a glass but alkaline-forming in the body. What matters is the ash the food leaves after the body metabolises it: acidic or alkaline.

Acid-forming foods are those that, once digested, produce acidic residues the body must neutralise. The main categories are: animal products — meat, fish, eggs, dairy; refined grains — white flour, white rice, most commercial bread and pasta; refined sugar in all forms; processed and packaged foods; alcohol; and most pharmaceutical drugs. These are not all equally harmful, and small amounts of some are managed easily by a healthy body. But consumed as the foundation of the daily diet, they push the body's terrain toward chronic acidity.

Understanding this is not about fear or rigid rules. It is about understanding the direction each food choice takes you — so that the cumulative effect of daily eating supports the body rather than burdens it.

The Alkaline Food List

Alkaline-forming foods are, overwhelmingly, the foods that nature made in their original form — leafy greens, fruits, roots, seeds, and herbs. The alkaline plate is not a diet of deprivation. It is a return to abundance.

Among the most alkaline-forming foods: dark leafy greens — kale, watercress, dandelion greens, wild arugula; fruits — figs, dates, mangoes, papayas, berries, melons; vegetables — cucumber, courgette, peppers, avocado, sweet potato; sea vegetables — dulse, wakame, nori; nuts and seeds — hemp seeds, sesame seeds and tahini, walnuts; whole grains — spelt, teff, quinoa; and herbs — elderflower, burdock root, sea moss, sarsaparilla.

Water deserves special mention. The body is roughly 60% water and the quality of what you hydrate with directly influences your internal environment. Natural spring water, naturally alkaline, is what the body was designed to receive. Filtered water is preferable to unfiltered tap water. Staying well hydrated with clean water is itself a foundational alkaline practice.

Plant Protein and the Protein Myth

One of the most persistent objections to alkaline eating is the protein question. "Where do you get your protein?" The answer is: from the same place the strongest animals on earth get theirs.

Gorillas, elephants, horses, and oxen are among the most muscular, powerful creatures alive. They eat plants. Hemp seeds contain all essential amino acids in excellent proportions. So do quinoa, teff, and combinations of whole plant foods eaten across a day. The alkaline tradition has always maintained that the body can build and maintain itself fully on plant-based sources — provided those plants are natural, mineral-rich, and varied.

The belief that animal protein is essential is relatively recent and culturally specific. Traditional healing communities across Africa, Asia, and the Americas lived on predominantly plant-based diets and demonstrated robust health and longevity. The protein they obtained was alkaline-forming — it came without the acid-producing phosphorus load, saturated fat, and inflammatory compounds that accompany animal protein.

What You Drink Matters

The body is predominantly water. What you hydrate with is not secondary to what you eat — it is equally fundamental. Clean, natural water is one of the most alkalising things you can give your body.

Natural spring water is slightly alkaline by nature — the minerals it picks up as it moves through rock give it a gentle alkalising quality. Tap water in many urban areas contains chlorine (added to kill bacteria), fluoride (added historically for dental health), and residues of medications and agricultural chemicals that pass through treatment systems. These compounds are not neutral in the body and add to the acid-forming burden it must manage.

Beyond water: fizzy drinks are among the most acid-forming substances a person can consume — phosphoric acid in cola drinks has a pH close to battery acid. Alcohol, coffee, and commercial fruit juices (loaded with refined sugar) all create acid-forming conditions. Herbal teas — elderflower, burdock, dandelion — are among the most alkalising drinks available and double as medicine.

Inflammation Is Acidity Speaking

Modern medicine calls it inflammation. The healing tradition calls it the body's response to an acidic internal environment. They are describing the same process from different angles — and the alkaline diet addresses the root.

Chronic inflammation underlies most of the conditions that dominate modern medicine: arthritis, eczema, psoriasis, asthma, IBS, Crohn's disease, type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and many cancers. These are not separate diseases with separate causes — they are different expressions of a body whose internal terrain has been pushed into a state of chronic stress and defence.

When the acid-forming diet is removed and replaced with mineral-rich, alkaline-forming whole food, inflammation decreases — not as a theory, but as a measurable, lived experience. People who shift to alkaline eating consistently report reductions in joint pain, improved skin conditions, reduced digestive inflammation, and clearer breathing. The body, given the environment it was designed for, begins to repair.

08 / 09

Building the Alkaline Plate

Alkaline eating is not about perfection. It is about direction. The aim is to make the majority of what you eat each day alkaline-forming — and to minimise, not necessarily eliminate, what is acid-forming.

A practical starting point: make half your plate at every meal dark leafy greens or vegetables. Add a serving of fruit daily — fresh figs, mango, papaya, or berries. Replace refined grains with spelt, teff, or quinoa. Swap cow's milk for hemp, walnut, or coconut milk. Replace fizzy drinks and commercial juices with spring water, coconut water, and herbal teas. Add sea moss and other sea vegetables weekly.

These shifts do not require a dramatic overnight transformation. They require a direction. Each meal is a choice between more acidic or more alkaline. Over days, weeks, and months, the cumulative shift in internal terrain produces changes that are felt in energy levels, skin clarity, joint comfort, digestion, mood, and immunity. The body responds to being given what it was designed to receive.

Practice this week

Add one genuinely alkaline food to each day this week — not instead of something, just in addition. Sea moss gel, fresh cucumber, leafy greens, or a glass of spring water with lime. Seven days, one addition per day. At the end of the week, note any change in energy levels, digestion, or the quality of sleep. Small additions reveal a great deal.

Monthly: track your mucus production and inflammatory symptoms (congestion, skin reactions, joint stiffness, digestive discomfort) for one month while increasing alkaline foods. You are not running a clinical trial. You are paying attention to how your specific body responds to specific inputs. Your body’s response is data.
